I originally graduated high school in 2007 and went college at UMass Dartmouth graduation for computer engineering right out of school. In my second semester of my freshman year I had difficulty moving my left arm which led to a diagnosis of multiple sclerosis. Between the difficulty living away from home because it was hard to function on my own while not fully ambulatory, and trouble adjusting to the diagnosis I ended up leaving UMass Dartmouth and giving up on school for the time being.

Thankfully now I'm fully ambulatory and I honestly forget I have MS most of the time. With my health back under control I started back at Massasoit Community College while working full time and in 2013 I received my associate degree in computer programming in 2013. After getting my degree I continued working retail jobs while trying to improve my computer science skills in my spare time.

After 6 years I eventually started back at Bridgewater State University in the Spring of 2019 working towards a degree in computer science. Going slowly and taking only a few classes at a time at first while continuing to work full time. I eventually picked up a minor in statistics because I find the field of data science interesting.

This current semester should hopefully be my last. I'm hoping finding a career in the field of computer science isn't too difficult considering my technical, employment, and life experience.
